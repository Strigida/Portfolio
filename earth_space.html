Earth Space

A Virtual Reality Game

Earth Space is a Virtual Reality (VR) exploration game set thousands of years after a nuclear holocaust has forced humans to abandon a hypothetical Earth, in favor of living in space. The player has been chosen as the first person to set foot on Earth in 3,000 years, and is tasked with setting up base camp to re-colonize Earth.

This is a school project I created in Unity using Oculus Rift over the course of a year or so, including programming in C#, making 3D assets in Blender & Maya, creating textures in Blender & Photoshop, and making sound effects & music in Logic Pro X. It has a networked side-quest mode where players can meet each other in Earth Space.

Research and testing were also important to the creation of this demo. I had fun imagining what possible eco-systems would spring up in this kind of environment given enough time without humanity, so upon entering the game we notice that fungi and mushrooms have become a huge proportion of the eco-system. In order to develop this game world my research lead my creativity. Each mushroom in Earth Space is a whimsical version of a type of mushroom. For example, the tall purple mushrooms are a giant homage to Laccaria Amethystina, a mushroom common to temperate North America.

Moderated usability testing followed by surveys showed that participants were quite drawn into the VR world around them, and were interest in the further content that would have been associated with a fully built game. Overall, 83.3 % of test subjects found the VR menu system easy to navigate and understand, whereas only 66.7% were able to easily identify & use intractable game objects such as buttons to open doors. This seems to suggest that traditional game interactions (like menus & stick navigation) are more easily understood by players than the new kinds of interaction that VR game worlds provide. Feedback from test subjects showed that 66% would have enjoyed more exposition or story development, as well as even more interactivity. Popular suggestions for types of interactivity included developing further relationships with the animals on the planet (33%), as well as having a detailed world building mode (50%).
